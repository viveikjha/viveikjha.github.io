Classical Mechanics — Reference Module

Introduction

Classical mechanics, in its most refined form, transcends Newtonian intuitions to embrace the elegance of analytical formulations. This module follows the canonical structure laid out in Goldstein, systematically progressing through the Lagrangian and Hamiltonian frameworks, variational principles, and symplectic structures that underpin modern theoretical physics.

Core Topics

  • Review of Newtonian Mechanics: Inertial frames, laws of motion, conservation principles, constraints and degrees of freedom.
  • Generalised Coordinates: Holonomic and non-holonomic systems, virtual displacements, d’Alembert’s principle.
  • Lagrangian Mechanics: Principle of least action, Euler–Lagrange equations, symmetries and Noether’s theorem.
  • Small Oscillations: Normal modes, coupled oscillators, eigenvalue problems.
  • Rigid Body Motion: Euler angles, inertia tensor, Euler’s equations, rotation about principal axes.
  • Non-inertial Frames: Coriolis and centrifugal forces, fictitious forces in rotating frames.
  • Central Force Problems: Keplerian motion, scattering, stability analysis.
  • Hamiltonian Mechanics: Legendre transformations, Hamilton’s equations, phase space formulation.
  • Canonical Transformations: Generating functions, Poisson brackets, Liouville’s theorem.
  • Hamilton–Jacobi Theory: H–J equation, action-angle variables, integrable systems.
  • Special Topics (Optional): Relativistic mechanics, classical field theory preliminaries, chaos and nonlinear dynamics.

Authoritative Textbooks

  • Classical Mechanics by Herbert Goldstein, Charles Poole, and John Safko — The definitive graduate-level text.
  • Mechanics by L.D. Landau and E.M. Lifshitz — Elegant and concise, with deep theoretical insight.
  • Analytical Mechanics by Fowles & Cassiday — Accessible, yet rigorous undergraduate text.
  • Classical Dynamics of Particles and Systems by Marion & Thornton — Practical examples with sound mathematics.
  • Mathematical Methods of Classical Mechanics by V.I. Arnold — For a symplectic and geometrical treatment.
