Electrodynamics — Reference Module

Introduction

Electrodynamics forms the backbone of classical physics, encompassing both static and dynamic electric and magnetic fields. This module covers foundational concepts of electricity and magnetism, extending to the full formalism of electrodynamics, including electromagnetic waves and classical optics such as polarisation phenomena.

Core Topics: Electricity and Magnetism (Paper 1)

  • Electrostatics: Coulomb’s law, electric field and potential, Gauss’s law, Laplace and Poisson equations.
  • Electric Dipoles and Multipoles: Dipole moment, potential due to multipole expansions.
  • Electric Current and Circuits: Current density, Ohm’s law, Kirchhoff’s laws, resistors, capacitors, and inductors.
  • Magnetostatics: Biot–Savart law, Ampère’s law, magnetic field of steady currents, magnetic dipole moment.
  • Magnetic Materials: Diamagnetism, paramagnetism, ferromagnetism, hysteresis.

Core Topics: Electrodynamics and Classical Optics (Paper 2)

  • Maxwell’s Equations: Differential and integral forms, displacement current, boundary conditions.
  • Electromagnetic Waves: Wave equations in vacuum and media, plane waves, Poynting vector, energy transport.
  • Potential Formulation: Scalar and vector potentials, gauge transformations, Lorenz and Coulomb gauges.
  • Radiation from Accelerated Charges: Larmor formula, dipole radiation, multipole radiation.
  • Waveguides and Transmission Lines: TEM, TE, TM modes, impedance matching.
  • Classical Optics: Reflection, refraction, polarisation, birefringence, optical activity, Jones calculus.
  • Relativistic Electrodynamics (Introductory): Electromagnetic field tensor, invariants, Lorentz transformations of fields.

Authoritative Textbooks

  • Introduction to Electrodynamics by David J. Griffiths — A lucid and rigorous undergraduate classic.
  • Classical Electrodynamics by John D. Jackson — The definitive advanced treatise, indispensable for mastery.
  • Electromagnetic Waves and Radiating Systems by Edward C. Jordan and Keith G. Balmain — For waveguides and radiation.
  • Principles of Optics by Max Born and Emil Wolf — The canonical text on classical optics and polarisation.
  • Classical Electricity and Magnetism by Panofsky and Phillips — A concise and elegant exposition.
