Quantum Mechanics — Reference Module

Introduction

Quantum mechanics, the most profound leap in theoretical physics since Newton, reveals a reality governed by probabilities and amplitudes, superpositions and symmetries. This module follows the pedagogical clarity of Griffiths, complemented by Zettili’s comprehensive problem-based perspective, to provide a complete intellectual toolkit for mastering non-relativistic quantum theory.

Core Topics

  • Mathematical Foundations: Complex vector spaces, Hilbert space, operators, eigenfunctions, Dirac notation.
  • Stern-Gerlach & Spin-½ Systems: Experimental motivation, spin matrices, Pauli algebra.
  • The Schrödinger Equation: Time-dependent and time-independent forms, boundary conditions, probability current.
  • 1D Potential Problems: Infinite and finite square wells, delta-function potentials, harmonic oscillator (algebraic and analytic methods).
  • Formalism of Quantum Mechanics: Postulates, observables, expectation values, uncertainty principle, Ehrenfest theorem.
  • Quantum Mechanics in 3D: Central potentials, angular momentum, spherical harmonics, hydrogen atom.
  • Spin and Addition of Angular Momentum: Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, coupled spin systems.
  • Identical Particles: Symmetrisation, antisymmetrisation, bosons and fermions.
  • Time-Independent Perturbation Theory: Non-degenerate and degenerate cases, applications to Stark and Zeeman effects.
  • Time-Dependent Perturbation Theory: Transition probabilities, Fermi’s Golden Rule, selection rules.
  • Scattering Theory (Introductory): Cross-section, Born approximation (optional, advanced).

Authoritative Textbooks

  • Introduction to Quantum Mechanics by David J. Griffiths — Clear, pedagogical, and widely adopted.
  • Quantum Mechanics: Concepts and Applications by Nouredine Zettili — Practice-focused, with extensive worked problems.
  • Principles of Quantum Mechanics by R. Shankar — Mathematically rigorous, logically structured.
  • Modern Quantum Mechanics by J.J. Sakurai — Advanced perspective, excellent for later mastery.
  • Quantum Physics by Stephen Gasiorowicz — Excellent supplementary resource for conceptual grounding.
