Mathematical Physics — Reference Module

Introduction

Mathematical physics is the rigorous application of advanced mathematical methods to problems in physics. It bridges abstract theory and concrete physical phenomena, providing the language and tools to formulate and solve equations fundamental to physical laws.

Core Topics

  • Linear Algebra: Vector spaces, matrices, eigenvalues and eigenvectors, diagonalisation.
  • Calculus of Several Variables: Partial derivatives, multiple integrals, gradient, divergence, curl.
  • Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs): First and second order ODEs, linear systems, boundary value problems.
  • Partial Differential Equations (PDEs): Wave, heat, Laplace and Poisson equations; separation of variables.
  • Complex Analysis: Analytic functions, contour integration, residue theorem, conformal mapping.
  • Fourier Analysis: Fourier series and transforms, applications to solving PDEs.
  • Special Functions: Legendre, Bessel, Hermite, Laguerre, Chebyshev polynomials.
  • Vector Calculus & Tensor Analysis: Divergence theorem, Stokes’ theorem, introduction to tensors.
  • Variational Principles and Calculus of Variations: Euler-Lagrange equations, Hamilton's principle.
  • Group Theory: Symmetry groups in physics, Lie groups and algebras basics.

Recommended Textbooks

  • Mathematical Methods for Physicists by George B. Arfken, Hans J. Weber & Frank E. Harris — A comprehensive and classical text.
  • Mathematical Methods in the Physical Sciences by Mary L. Boas — Clear and accessible introduction.
  • Mathematics for Physics by Michael Stone and Paul Goldbart — Emphasises physical intuition behind the math.
  • Mathematical Physics by Eugene Butkov — Standard undergraduate-level text covering core methods.
  • Special Functions and Their Applications by N. N. Lebedev — For detailed treatment of special functions.
