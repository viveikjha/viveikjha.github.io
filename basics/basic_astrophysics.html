Basics of Astrophysics — Reference Module

This module lays the groundwork for understanding celestial mechanics, stellar physics, observational techniques, and key cosmic processes. It is modelled on the lucid expositions of Arnab Rai Choudhuri and the comprehensive scope of Fundamental Astronomy by Karttunen & Oja.

Core Topics

  • Celestial Sphere & Coordinates: Equatorial, ecliptic, galactic systems
  • Timekeeping: Sidereal time, Julian date, precession, nutation
  • Telescopes & Detectors: Optical, radio, X-ray telescopes; CCDs, photometry
  • Stellar Properties: HR diagram, classification, distance indicators
  • Radiation Mechanisms: Blackbody, line spectra, synchrotron, bremsstrahlung
  • Stellar Structure: Hydrostatic equilibrium, energy transport, nuclear fusion
  • Binary Stars: Mass functions, eclipsing binaries, spectroscopic binaries
  • Compact Objects: White dwarfs, neutron stars, black holes (introductory)
  • Galactic Architecture: Milky Way structure, rotation curve, halo, bulge
  • Cosmic Distance Ladder: Parallax to redshift scaling

Key References

  • Astrophysics for Physicists by Arnab Rai Choudhuri
  • Fundamental Astronomy by Hannu Karttunen, Pekka Kröger, Heikki Oja et al.
  • An Introduction to Modern Astrophysics by Carroll & Ostlie (selective reading)
  • Observational Astrophysics by Pierre Léna
