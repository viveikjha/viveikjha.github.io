Statistical Mechanics — Reference Module

Introduction

Statistical mechanics is the discipline that interprets thermodynamics through the lens of microscopic laws. By embracing probability theory and ensemble averages, it accounts for the emergent phenomena of heat, pressure, entropy, and phase transitions. The structure here closely follows the elegant treatment by Pathria, enriched by Kerson Huang’s lucid insights.

Core Topics

  • Fundamentals of Probability Theory: Ensembles, random variables, laws of large numbers, central limit theorem.
  • Classical Thermodynamics Recap: Laws of thermodynamics, thermodynamic potentials, Legendre transforms.
  • Microcanonical Ensemble: Entropy, Boltzmann relation, Liouville's theorem, ergodic hypothesis.
  • Canonical Ensemble: Partition function, thermodynamic quantities, equipartition theorem, fluctuations.
  • Grand Canonical Ensemble: Chemical potential, particle number fluctuations, ideal quantum gases.
  • Classical Statistical Mechanics: Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution, classical ideal gas, Gibbs paradox.
  • Quantum Statistical Mechanics: Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac statistics, degenerate quantum gases.
  • Blackbody Radiation: Planck distribution, photon gas, radiation pressure.
  • Bose-Einstein Condensation: Critical temperature, condensate fraction, real-world analogues.
  • Fermi Gas: Free electron model, density of states, specific heat of metals.
  • Interacting Systems and Mean Field Theory: Ising model, Bragg-Williams approximation, van der Waals gas.
  • Phase Transitions and Critical Phenomena: Order parameters, Landau theory, critical exponents (introductory).

Authoritative Textbooks

  • Statistical Mechanics by R.K. Pathria and Paul D. Beale — Comprehensive and rigorous.
  • Statistical Physics by Kerson Huang — Intuitive, with elegant physical arguments.
  • Fundamentals of Statistical and Thermal Physics by F. Reif — For conceptual grounding.
  • Equilibrium Statistical Physics by Michael Plischke & Birger Bergersen — A solid alternative treatment.
  • Statistical Mechanics by Landau and Lifshitz — For advanced and formal exposition.
