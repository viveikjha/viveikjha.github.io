Galaxy Evolution — Reference Module

Galaxy evolution examines how galaxies form, grow, transform, and die across cosmic time. This module synthesises classical treatments (e.g., Schneider) with state-of-the-art observational and theoretical advances from major surveys and simulations.

Core Topics

  • Galactic Classification: Hubble sequence, morphology, colour–mass relations.
  • Stellar Populations: Formation epochs, population synthesis, metal enrichment.
  • Star Formation History: Global SFRD, quenching processes, rejuvenation.
  • Gas Content and ISM: HI and H₂ evolution, ionisation, dust, and cooling.
  • Dark Matter Halos: Halo-galaxy connection, abundance matching, clustering.
  • Mergers and Interactions: Morphological disturbances, gas inflow, induced starbursts.
  • Feedback: SN and AGN-driven feedback, halo cycling, CGM properties.
  • Environmental Effects: Group/cluster evolution, ram pressure, tidal stripping.
  • High-z Galaxies: JWST-era formation snapshots, early massive galaxies, reionisation.

Key References

  • Extragalactic Astronomy and Cosmology by Peter Schneider
  • Galaxy Formation and Evolution by Mo, van den Bosch, and White
  • Principles of Physical Cosmology by P. J. E. Peebles
  • Selected ARA&A reviews on star formation, quenching, and mass assembly
