AGN Physics — Reference Module

Overview

Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN) represent the most energetic long-lived phenomena in the Universe, powered by accretion onto supermassive black holes. The subject draws together high-energy astrophysics, radiation processes, plasma dynamics, and galaxy evolution. This module is structured around the authoritative treatments of Hagai Netzer and Julian Krolik, offering both conceptual clarity and quantitative rigour.

Core Topics

  • AGN Classification: Seyferts, QSOs, Blazars, LINERs; the unification model.
  • Central Engine: Supermassive black holes, Eddington limit, accretion efficiency.
  • Accretion Discs: Thin disc model, Shakura–Sunyaev viscosity, radiative transfer within discs.
  • Broad and Narrow Line Regions: Ionisation, line widths, reverberation mapping.
  • Radiation Mechanisms: Bremsstrahlung, synchrotron, Compton scattering, line emission.
  • X-ray Emission: Coronae, reflection spectra, Fe Kα line diagnostics.
  • Obscuration and Tori: Dusty torus, type 1 vs type 2 AGNs, IR diagnostics.
  • Jets and Radio Emission: Relativistic jets, synchrotron emission, FR-I/II classification.
  • AGN Feedback: Mechanical and radiative modes, galaxy–SMBH co-evolution, quenching.
  • AGN Demographics: Luminosity functions, cosmic downsizing, black hole mass functions.

Key References

  • The Physics and Evolution of Active Galactic Nuclei by Hagai Netzer — lucid and comprehensive.
  • Active Galactic Nuclei: From the Central Black Hole to the Galactic Environment by Julian Krolik — rigorous and foundational.
  • AGN Physics lecture series by Bradley Peterson (Ohio State) — excellent pedagogical clarity.
  • High Energy Astrophysics by Malcolm Longair — for background on jets, X-rays, and radiative processes.
